# Advanced ML Ensemble Architecture Workflow

This workflow transitions the Phantom Consensus system from a pure rule-based script into a **Machine Learning Ensemble Model**. By splitting the logic into specialized "Engines" (Base Models) and combining them using a Meta-Aggregator (Stacking/Bagging), we can make far more nuanced decisions.

```mermaid
    %% Meta Model
    subgraph Layer 2: Aggregator (Meta-Model)
        Meta{Meta-Aggregator Engine\n(Stacking / Bagging)}
    end

    %% Output
    Meta -- Final Votes --> Out[Final Agreement\n(Proposals & Supporters)]
```

## ⚙️ The 3 Base Engines

### Engine 1: Stakeholder & Political Engine
**Goal:** Evaluate the political landscape and relationship dynamics.
*   **Inputs:** `df_reps`, `df_rels`
*   **Features Extracted:** 
    *   `faction_support_ratio`: How much of a faction supports a proposal.
    *   `trust_network_centrality`: Is the sponsor well-trusted by high-influence peers?
    *   `betrayal_hazard`: Aggregate risk of betrayal among potential supporters.
*   **Output:** `political_score` for each proposal and representative.

### Engine 2: Budget & Constraints Engine
**Goal:** Evaluate the practical and economic viability of proposals.
*   **Inputs:** `df_props` (specifically `budget_estimate`, `priority`, `category`)
*   **Features Extracted:**
    *   `cost_to_priority_ratio`: Is the `budget_estimate` (e.g., 4,200,000) justified by a high `priority`?
    *   `budget_availability`: Penalize proposals if their budget is disproportionately high or missing.
    *   `category_weight`: Balance the selection (e.g., don't pick 3 "finance" proposals; mix with "infrastructure").
*   **Output:** `economic_score` for each proposal.

### Engine 3: NLP & Semantic Risk Engine (The "One More Engine")
**Goal:** Read between the lines of the text fields.
*   **Inputs:** `df_objs['reason']`, `df_props['notes']`
*   **Features Extracted:**
    *   `sentiment_polarity`: Use NLP (TextBlob/VADER) on objection reasons. Highly negative words ("Dangerous", "Catastrophic") increase severity exponentially.
    *   `semantic_clustering`: If 3 reps say "Too costly", "Fiscal concerns", and "Budgetary risk", cluster them into an "Economic Poison Pill" flag.
*   **Output:** `nlp_risk_score` for each proposal.

## 🏗️ The Meta-Aggregator Engine (Bagging / Stacking)

Once the 3 base engines produce their scores, we don't just average them. We use an **ML Aggregator** to decide the final consensus.

### Approach 1: Stacking (Recommended for this hackathon)
A **Stacking Classifier** (like Logistic Regression or XGBoost) learns *how* to weigh the 3 engines.
*   *Example:* The model might learn that if the NLP Engine flags a "Security Risk" (Engine 3), it should veto the proposal entirely, even if the Political Engine (Engine 1) says it's highly supported.

### Approach 2: Bagging (Random Forest/Voting Classifier)
A **Voting Classifier** (Soft Voting) treats the 3 engines as a committee. 
*   *Example:* Engine 1 votes 0.8, Engine 2 votes 0.6, Engine 3 votes 0.9. The average probability is 0.76.
